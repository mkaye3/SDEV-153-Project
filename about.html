What Are the Current Laws and Why Do They Need Changed

Current traffic laws are not doing enough to deter dangerous behaviors such as speeding, driving under the influence, and using a phone while behind the wheel. Although these actions are widely recognized as serious risks, many drivers continue to engage in them because the penalties are often not severe enough to create meaningful consequences. Speeding significantly reduces reaction time and increases the severity of crashes, putting not only the driver but also passengers, pedestrians, and other motorists in danger. Distracted driving, especially when a driver is holding or looking at a phone, takes attention away from the road for even a few seconds—more than enough time for a life-altering accident to occur. Most concerning is driving under the influence, which remains one of the leading causes of preventable injuries and fatalities despite ongoing awareness campaigns and existing laws.

The current system often allows repeat offenders to continue driving with minimal interruption, and penalties such as small fines or short-term license suspensions fail to reflect the seriousness of these offenses. In many cases, the punishment does not match the potential harm caused, which weakens the law’s ability to discourage reckless behavior. Stronger and more consistent enforcement, combined with harsher penalties—such as increased fines, longer license suspensions, mandatory education or rehabilitation programs, and stricter consequences for repeat offenses—would send a clear message that these actions are unacceptable. Updating and strengthening traffic laws is necessary to better protect the public, reduce preventable accidents, and create safer roads for everyone.
